# South Seas Mango Halibut: A Tropical Paradise on Your Plate


Transport yourself to a sun-drenched tropical paradise with this exquisite South Seas Mango Halibut recipe. This dish beautifully marries the delicate flavor of fresh halibut with the sweet tropical notes of ripe mango, creating a harmonious blend that's both sophisticated and refreshing. While we often associate seafood with lemon and herbs, this unique preparation takes inspiration from Pacific Island cuisine, where fruit-based preparations bring out the best in fresh fish. Just like how [tropical smoothie bowls](https://healtystack.com/plant-based-smoothie-bowls/) can brighten your morning, this dish brings sunshine to your dinner table.



## Ingredients




    Ingredient
    Amount


    Fresh halibut fillets
    4 (6-ounce) pieces


    Ripe mangoes
    2 large


    Coconut milk
    1 cup


    Fresh ginger
    2 tablespoons, minced


    Garlic
    3 cloves, minced


    Lime juice
    2 tablespoons


    Green onions
    4, finely chopped


    Coconut oil
    3 tablespoons


    Red pepper flakes
    1/2 teaspoon


    Sea salt
    1 teaspoon


    Black pepper
    1/2 teaspoon


    Fresh cilantro
    1/4 cup, chopped


    Brown sugar
    1 tablespoon





## Instructions


### Step 1: Prepare the Mango Sauce

Begin by peeling and dicing one mango into small chunks. In a blender, combine the mango pieces, 1/2 cup coconut milk, 1 tablespoon ginger, 1 clove minced garlic, 1 tablespoon lime juice, and 1/2 tablespoon brown sugar. Blend until smooth and set aside. This vibrant sauce will be the star of our dish, much like how fresh ingredients make [healthy pizzas](https://healtystack.com/healthy-vegan-pizza/) truly special.



### Step 2: Marinate the Halibut

In a shallow dish, combine remaining coconut milk, ginger, garlic, lime juice, and brown sugar. Add the halibut fillets and let them marinate for 30 minutes in the refrigerator. This will infuse the fish with tropical flavors while maintaining its delicate texture.



### Step 3: Prepare the Mango Salsa

While the fish marinates, dice the remaining mango into small cubes. Combine with half of the chopped green onions, 2 tablespoons cilantro, red pepper flakes, and a splash of lime juice. This fresh salsa adds color and texture to the final dish, similar to how fresh toppings enhance [plant-based tacos](https://healtystack.com/plant-based-tacos/).



### Step 4: Cook the Halibut

Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Remove halibut from marinade and pat dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper. Cook the fillets for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through.



### Step 5: Prepare the Plate

Warm the mango sauce gently in a small saucepan. Pour a pool of sauce onto each plate, place a halibut fillet in the center, and top with the fresh mango salsa. Garnish with remaining green onions and cilantro.

## Frequently Asked Questions


### Can I use frozen halibut for this recipe?

While fresh halibut is preferred, you can use frozen halibut. Just ensure it's completely thawed and patted dry before cooking. The texture might be slightly different, but the dish will still be delicious.



### What can I substitute for coconut milk?

If you don't have coconut milk, you can use a combination of heavy cream and a touch of coconut extract. For a lighter version, unsweetened almond milk with a splash of coconut extract works well too.



### How do I know when the halibut is perfectly cooked?

The halibut is done when it flakes easily with a fork and is opaque throughout. Be careful not to overcook as halibut can become dry quickly. The internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C).
