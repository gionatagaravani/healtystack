# Festive Holiday Popcorn Mix: A Sweet and Savory Christmas Treat


The holiday season calls for special treats that bring joy and festive cheer to our gatherings. This Holiday Popcorn Mix is the perfect combination of sweet, salty, and crunchy elements that create an irresistible snack perfect for holiday parties, movie nights, or as homemade gifts. Much like our traditional [holiday cookies](https://healtystack.com/gingerbread-cookie-decorating/), this popcorn mix brings a festive touch to your celebration, but with a modern twist.



## Ingredients


    
        Ingredient
        Amount
    
    
        Plain popcorn kernels
        1/2 cup (makes about 12 cups popped)
    
    
        White chocolate chips
        2 cups
    
    
        Red and green candy melts
        1 cup each
    
    
        Mini pretzels
        2 cups
    
    
        M&Ms (red and green)
        1 cup
    
    
        Mixed nuts (optional)
        1 cup
    
    
        Holiday sprinkles
        1/2 cup
    
    
        Coconut oil
        2 tablespoons
    




## Instructions


### Step 1: Popping the Popcorn

Begin by popping the kernels using your preferred method. I recommend using an air popper or stovetop method for the freshest taste. Avoid using microwave popcorn as it often contains additional butter and salt that might interfere with our flavoring. Once popped, spread the popcorn on a large baking sheet lined with parchment paper, removing any unpopped kernels.



### Step 2: Preparing the Chocolate

In separate microwave-safe bowls, melt the white chocolate chips and candy melts with a small amount of coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval to prevent burning. The coconut oil helps create a smoother consistency and prevents the chocolate from becoming too thick.



### Step 3: Creating the Base Mix

In a large bowl, combine the popped popcorn with pretzels and nuts (if using). Ensure even distribution of all components. This combination creates a perfect base for our holiday mix, similar to how we layer flavors in our [holiday trifle](https://healtystack.com/holiday-trifle-variations/).



### Step 4: Adding the Chocolate

Drizzle the melted white chocolate over the popcorn mixture, stirring gently to coat evenly. Work quickly as the chocolate will begin to set. Next, drizzle the red and green candy melts in a decorative pattern across the mixture.



### Step 5: Final Touches

Before the chocolate sets completely, sprinkle the M&Ms and holiday sprinkles over the mixture. The warmth from the melted chocolate will help them adhere to the popcorn and pretzels. For an extra festive touch, you might want to add some edible glitter, similar to what we use in our [festive cheesecake decorations](https://healtystack.com/festive-cheesecake-recipe/).



### Step 6: Setting and Storage

Allow the mixture to set completely at room temperature, which typically takes about 30 minutes. Once set, break into clusters and store in an airtight container. The mix will stay fresh for up to two weeks at room temperature.

## Frequently Asked Questions


### Can I make this popcorn mix ahead of time?

Yes, you can make this mix up to two weeks in advance. Store it in an airtight container at room temperature. If your house is particularly warm, consider storing it in a cool, dry place to prevent the chocolate from melting.



### What's the best way to package this mix for gifts?

Use cellophane bags tied with festive ribbons, or mason jars decorated with holiday themes. Make sure the container is airtight to maintain freshness. Add a personalized label with the date made and ingredients list, especially if giving as gifts.



### Can I customize the ingredients?

Absolutely! Feel free to swap out nuts for different varieties, use dark chocolate instead of white, or add other treats like dried cranberries or coconut flakes. Just maintain similar proportions to ensure the chocolate properly coats all ingredients.
