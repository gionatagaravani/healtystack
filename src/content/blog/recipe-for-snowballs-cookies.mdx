Snowball cookies are a beloved holiday tradition that brings joy to every cookie platter. These delicate, buttery cookies are made with finely chopped nuts and rolled twice in powdered sugar to create their signature snowy appearance. Whether you know them as Russian Tea Cakes, Mexican Wedding Cookies, or Italian Wedding Cookies, these melt-in-your-mouth treats are sure to become a family favorite.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **Simple Ingredients**: Uses basic pantry staples
- **No Eggs Required**: Perfect for those with egg allergies
- **Make-Ahead Friendly**: Dough and baked cookies freeze well
- **Versatile**: Can be made with different nuts
- **Impressive Presentation**: Beautiful snowy appearance
- **Kid-Friendly**: Fun to roll in powdered sugar

## Essential Ingredients

- 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
- 1/2 cup powdered sugar (plus extra for rolling)
- 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 3/4 cup finely chopped nuts (pecans, walnuts, or almonds)

## Kitchen Equipment Needed

- Stand mixer or hand mixer
- Large mixing bowl
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Baking sheets
- Parchment paper or silicone baking mats
- Wire cooling rack
- Small bowl for rolling in powdered sugar
- Food processor (optional, for chopping nuts)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Dough

1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C)
2. Line baking sheets with parchment paper
3. In a large bowl or stand mixer:
   - Cream butter until light and fluffy (2-3 minutes)
   - Add 1/2 cup powdered sugar
   - Beat until well combined
   - Mix in vanilla extract

4. In a separate bowl, whisk together:
   - Flour
   - Salt

5. Gradually add dry ingredients to butter mixture:
   - Mix until just combined
   - Fold in chopped nuts
   - Do not overmix

### 2. Shape and Bake

1. Roll dough into 1-inch balls
2. Place on prepared baking sheets
3. Bake for 12-15 minutes until:
   - Bottoms are lightly golden
   - Tops remain pale
   - Cookies are set but not browned

### 3. The Double Roll

1. First Roll (When Warm):
   - Let cookies cool for 5 minutes
   - Roll in powdered sugar while still warm
   - Place on wire rack to cool completely

2. Second Roll (When Cool):
   - Once completely cool
   - Roll again in powdered sugar
   - This creates the perfect snowy coating

## Pro Tips for Perfect Snowballs

1. **Butter Temperature**: Should be soft but still cool to touch
2. **Nut Preparation**: Chop nuts very finely for best texture
3. **Dough Consistency**: If too crumbly, add 1-2 teaspoons milk
4. **Size Matters**: Keep cookies uniform for even baking
5. **Sugar Coating**: Don't skip the double roll in powdered sugar

## Variations and Substitutions

- **Nut Options**: 
  - Traditional pecans
  - Walnuts for earthier flavor
  - Almonds for subtle sweetness
  - Hazelnuts for unique taste

- **Flavor Additions**:
  - 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  - Lemon or orange zest
  - Mini chocolate chips
  - Ground cinnamon

- **Gluten-Free Version**:
  - Use 1:1 gluten-free flour blend
  - Add 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum if blend doesn't include it

## Storage and Make-Ahead Tips

- **Room Temperature**: 
  - Store in airtight container
  - Last up to 1 week
  - Layer with wax paper

- **Freezer Storage**:
  - Baked cookies freeze well for up to 3 months
  - Wrap tightly in plastic wrap
  - Place in freezer bag
  - Re-roll in powdered sugar after thawing

- **Make-Ahead Dough**:
  - Can be refrigerated for up to 3 days
  - Freeze shaped balls for up to 3 months
  - Thaw in refrigerator before baking

## Common Troubleshooting Tips

1. **If Cookies Spread**:
   - Chill dough for 30 minutes
   - Check butter temperature
   - Ensure accurate flour measurement

2. **If Cookies Are Too Dry**:
   - Reduce baking time
   - Check flour measurement
   - Add 1-2 teaspoons milk to dough

3. **If Sugar Coating Is Uneven**:
   - Ensure cookies are still warm for first roll
   - Wait until completely cool for second roll
   - Use fresh powdered sugar

## Recipe FAQs

**Q: Why did my cookies flatten?**
A: Usually due to too-soft butter or warm dough. Chill dough if needed.

**Q: Can I make these without nuts?**
A: Yes, though nuts provide structure. Replace with more flour (about 1/4 cup).

**Q: Why aren't my cookies holding together?**
A: The dough might be too dry. Add 1-2 teaspoons milk if needed.

**Q: Can I use salted butter?**
A: Yes, but omit the additional salt in the recipe.

**Q: How do I know when they're done?**
A: Bottoms should be lightly golden, but tops should remain pale.
