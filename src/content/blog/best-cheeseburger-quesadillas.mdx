# Best Cheeseburger Quesadillas: The Perfect Fusion of Mexican and American Comfort Food


Imagine combining two of the most beloved comfort foods into one irresistible dish - that's exactly what cheeseburger quesadillas deliver! This creative fusion brings together the savory flavors of a classic American cheeseburger with the crispy, cheesy goodness of a Mexican quesadilla. Perfect for busy weeknights or casual entertaining, these quesadillas transform familiar ingredients into an exciting new meal that will have everyone asking for seconds. While traditional burger toppings are great, you might also want to experiment with different variations, similar to how you can modify classic dishes like a [vegetarian green bean casserole](https://healtystack.com/vegetarian-green-bean-casserole/) to suit different tastes.



## Ingredients




    Ingredient
    Amount


    Ground beef (80/20)
    1 pound


    Large flour tortillas
    8 (10-inch)


    Shredded cheddar cheese
    2 cups


    Diced onion
    1 medium


    Diced tomatoes
    2 medium


    Diced pickles
    1/2 cup


    Yellow mustard
    2 tablespoons


    Ketchup
    1/4 cup


    Mayonnaise
    1/4 cup


    Garlic powder
    1 teaspoon


    Onion powder
    1 teaspoon


    Salt
    1 teaspoon


    Black pepper
    1/2 teaspoon


    Butter for grilling
    4 tablespoons





## Step-by-Step Instructions


### 1. Prepare the Meat Mixture

Start by heating a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and break it up into small pieces using a wooden spoon. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Cook until the beef is nicely browned and no pink remains, about 7-8 minutes. Drain any excess fat, but leave a small amount for flavor. Just as you might prep ingredients ahead for a [make-ahead casserole](https://healtystack.com/make-ahead-green-bean-casserole/), you can prepare this meat mixture in advance.



### 2. Create the Special Sauce

In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, ketchup, and mustard. Mix well until thoroughly combined. This sauce will give your quesadillas that authentic cheeseburger taste.



### 3. Prepare the Vegetables

Ensure all your vegetables are diced into small, uniform pieces. This helps with even distribution and prevents the quesadilla from becoming too bulky. The onions should be about 1/4-inch dice, tomatoes seeded and diced, and pickles finely chopped.



### 4. Assembly

Lay out your tortillas on a clean work surface. Spread a thin layer of the special sauce on one half of each tortilla. On the same half, add a portion of the cooked ground beef, followed by a generous sprinkle of shredded cheese, diced onions, tomatoes, and pickles. Similar to how you might layer ingredients in a [slow cooker casserole](https://healtystack.com/slow-cooker-green-bean-casserole/), proper layering ensures even distribution of flavors.



### 5. Cooking the Quesadillas

Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a small pat of butter and let it melt. Fold each tortilla in half over the filling, pressing gently to seal. Place in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy, and the cheese has melted completely. Work in batches if necessary, adding more butter as needed.



### 6. Finishing Touches

Once cooked, transfer the quesadillas to a cutting board and let them rest for 1-2 minutes. This allows the cheese to set slightly and makes them easier to cut. Slice each quesadilla into 2-3 triangles, depending on size preference.

## FAQ


### Can I make these quesadillas ahead of time?

While it's best to serve quesadillas immediately while they're hot and crispy, you can prepare the components ahead of time. Cook the meat mixture, prepare the special sauce, and chop the vegetables up to 24 hours in advance. Store everything separately in the refrigerator, then assemble and cook the quesadillas just before serving.



### What's the best type of cheese to use?

While the recipe calls for cheddar cheese, you can experiment with different varieties. A mixture of cheddar and Monterey Jack works well, or try American cheese for that classic cheeseburger taste. Just make sure to use a cheese that melts well for the best results.
